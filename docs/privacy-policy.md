# Metro Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 5 June 2026_

Metro ("the app", "we", "us") is a privacy-first app for encrypted messaging,
a self-custodial wallet, and onchain governance. This policy explains what data
the app handles and how.

## Summary

- Your private keys never leave your device. We do not have access to them.
- Messages are end-to-end encrypted via the XMTP protocol. We cannot read them.
- We do not sell your data. We do not use your data for advertising or tracking.
- Most data stays on your device or transits open, encrypted protocols.

## Information the app handles

**Wallet address and onchain identity.** Your Ethereum wallet address is your
identity in Metro. It is used to send and receive messages, view balances, and
cast governance votes. Your private keys are generated and stored only on your
device in the operating system's secure storage.

**Messages and attachments.** Messages, images, voice recordings, and locations
you choose to send are end-to-end encrypted using the XMTP protocol and
delivered over the XMTP network. They are encrypted on your device before they
leave it; we cannot read their contents. Attachments you send are encrypted in
transit.

**Location.** Location data is handled only if you explicitly choose to share
your location in a chat. It is never collected in the background.

**Push notification token.** If you enable notifications, a push token (via
Firebase Cloud Messaging) is used to deliver message notifications to your
device. Notification content is minimized.

## How data is used

We handle the above data solely to provide the app's core functionality:
messaging, wallet, and governance. We do not use it for advertising, profiling,
or cross-app tracking, and we do not sell it.

## Third-party services

- **XMTP** — open, end-to-end-encrypted messaging protocol used to deliver
  messages. XMTP nodes relay encrypted message payloads.
- **Firebase Cloud Messaging (Google)** — used to deliver push notifications.
- **Public blockchain networks and Snapshot** — used to read balances/NFTs and
  to submit governance votes you initiate. Onchain activity is inherently
  public.

These services act as processors/relays for the functionality you request.

## Data retention and deletion

Data is self-custodial and primarily stored on your device. Uninstalling the
app removes local data from your device. Because your identity and messages are
controlled by your own keys (not by us), we do not hold an account to delete.
For questions or deletion requests, contact us at the email below.

## Children

Metro is not directed at children under 13.

## Security

We rely on device secure storage for keys, end-to-end encryption for messages,
and TLS for all network connections.
